How to install

iOS — all iPhones and similar devices from Apple

First you'll need to install an app called TestFlight, which is Apple's platform for testing apps not yet publicly listed in its App Store. You can find this by searching in the App Store on your iPhone, or by going to https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/testflight/id899247664

Once you've installed TestFlight, you can install Badger Watch by going to https://testflight.apple.com/join/aiSmVTU4 on your iPhone.

Please ensure your phone is set to update its apps automatically, so you can stay with the latest version:

  • In TestFlight, tap the profile picture or person-in-a-circle icon at the top right
  • Tap "automatic updates" and ensure it's set to "on"
  • Back on the main TestFlight screen, tap Badger Watch in the list, and on its screen ensure "automatic updates" is switched on

Android — nearly all other smartphones

On your phone, go to the app's Play Store listing at https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.projectbadger.badger. From there you should be able to install it.

Please ensure your phone is set to update its apps automatically, so you can stay with the latest version. If you need to check or change this:

  • Open the Play Store
  • Tap your user icon: a profile picture or letter in a circle in the top right of your screen
  • Tap "settings" in the menu
  • Tap "network preferences"
  • Tap "auto-update apps" and ensure it's set to "Wi-Fi only" or "any network" according to your preference

How to update manually

You can check your installed version against the notice on the main menu. If an update is rolled out before there's been a chance to update this site, you might briefly find you have a more recent version than is listed there, but if you have an older one, please update.

Sometimes the latest version available for Android and iOS may be out of sync for a day or two, but version numbers run in parallel on the two systems and should always correspond to the same intended functionality. If something works on one system but not the other, they're on the same version number, and this isn't listed as a known issue, please report a bug!

iOS

Your phone is most likely to do its automatic updates when it's connected to Wi-Fi and is plugged in to charge.

If your phone hasn't updated the app automatically:

  • Open the TestFlight app
  • Badger Watch should be listed along with its version number, and a button to "install", "update" or "open", so tap "update" if you see that.

Android

Your phone is most likely to do its automatic updates when it's connected to Wi-Fi and has been plugged in for a while to charge.

If your phone hasn't updated the app automatically:

  • Open the Play Store app
  • Tap your user icon: a profile picture or letter in a circle in the top right of your screen
  • Tap "manage apps and device" in the menu
  • If there's a section titled "all apps up to date" tap it and then tap the button "check for updates". If instead there's a section titled "updates available" tap "see details"
  • If Badger Watch is in the list, just tap "update", or tap on the app's icon or name to show a whole screen about it

On the screen about Badger Watch, however you've reached it, you can tap "what's new" or "about this app", scroll down and you should see a version number. This is the latest version available to you. If you know that a newer version has been released, you may just have to wait a few hours for it to show up on your device. Ensure your phone is on Wi-Fi and well charged, and that you don't have a problem such as full storage preventing your phone from installing updates.
